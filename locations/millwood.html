Frequently Asked Questions — Hardwood Floors in Millwood

What makes old-growth fir floors in Millwood homes special?

Old-growth Douglas fir from the early 20th century is denser and harder than modern-growth wood, with tight grain and extraordinary durability. Millwood's older homes often have this wood beneath carpet or layers of old finish. When properly refinished, these floors are striking and can last another century with proper care.

How do you refinish old-growth fir floors in Millwood without damaging them?

Old-growth fir requires a gentler sanding approach than oak. We use drum sanders carefully and often finish with orbital sanding to avoid cross-grain scratches. The wood takes stain beautifully but can also be finished natural to showcase its distinctive character.

Do you remove carpet to assess original hardwood in Millwood homes?

We will inspect existing flooring and often can determine what is underneath by checking closets or lifting a floor register without fully removing carpet. Free assessments are available — call us and we can schedule a time to evaluate your Millwood home.
