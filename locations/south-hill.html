Scratched, Dull, and Stained — But Not Beyond Saving

South Hill floors have seen a lot. The original Douglas fir in a 1920s Craftsman bungalow near Manito Park. The red oak in a 1950s ranch off High Drive. The wide-plank white oak in a Rockwood remodel that looked perfect three years ago and now looks tired. Refinishing removes all of it — scratches, dullness, water rings, worn traffic lanes — by sanding down to fresh wood and building a new finish from scratch.

What comes back looks like new installation, costs a fraction of replacement, and typically takes 2–3 days. If your South Hill floors have been bothering you, there's a strong chance they're refinishable and worth refinishing. The free estimate answers the key question: can your floors be saved, and what will it actually cost?

What Waiting Is Doing to Your Floors

  • Scratches deepen over time. Once the finish layer is broken, bare wood absorbs grit, moisture, and foot traffic oils. The damage that stops at the surface today won't stay there.
  • South Hill's historic floors are irreplaceable. Old-growth Douglas fir and early-cut red oak that came out of the same regional forests that built this city can't be sourced today. Every year of neglect removes refinishing options.
  • Pre-sale window is short. Refinishing requires cure time before furniture goes back and listings go live. Waiting until a showing is two weeks away means panic decisions and rushed work.
  • Replacement costs 3–4x more. Refinishing at $3.50–$7/sqft vs. new installation at $6–$12/sqft or more. When the wood underneath is solid, refinishing is almost always the right call.

How We Refinish Floors

1. Free in-home estimate. We assess the wood — species, thickness, condition, depth of damage — and give you a written quote. Full refinish, screen-and-recoat, or selective board replacement depending on what we find.

2. Stain samples on your actual floor. You pick the color on your floor, in your lighting — not from a card. Warm walnut, natural honey oak, cool gray, weathered driftwood — we apply test swatches and you approve before we commit.

3. Dustless sanding. Our HEPA-filtered containment system captures dust at the source. South Hill homes run forced-air heat for 6+ months a year — traditional refinishing without dust control sends wood particles into your ductwork for months. Our system prevents that entirely.

4. Stain, seal, and finish coats. Two to three coats of commercial-grade polyurethane with buffing between each. Water-based options cure faster; oil-based adds depth and warmth. We recommend what's right for your specific floor and usage.

5. Cure and handoff. Light foot traffic in 24 hours (water-based) or 48 hours (oil-based). We give you exact protocol for your finish — including how to manage humidity through Spokane's heating season to protect the new finish.

Quick Answers Before You Call

Will there be a lot of dust?

Not with our dustless sanding system. It captures the vast majority of sanding dust at the source. Dramatically different from traditional refinishing — most South Hill homeowners stay home during the project.

What if I choose the wrong stain color?

You don't choose from a card — you choose from test patches applied to your actual floor, in your actual light. You approve it before we proceed. No post-commitment regret.

How long until I can use the room?

Water-based finish: foot traffic in 24 hours, furniture in 72 hours, area rugs after 7 days. Oil-based: 48 hours for foot traffic, 7 days for furniture.

Do you work on the South Hill?

Yes. South Hill is one of our most active areas — we regularly work in Rockwood, Manito, Comstock, Lincoln Heights, and all surrounding neighborhoods.
