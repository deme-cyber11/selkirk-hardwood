FAQ

Questions Spokane Homeowners Ask Before Booking

Most refinish jobs take 2–3 days: one day for sanding, one for stain and first finish coat, one for final coats and dry time. Larger homes or jobs needing repair work may take 4–5 days. We give you a firm timeline at estimate so you can plan accordingly.

Not necessarily. Many customers stay home during sanding — our dustless system keeps the mess contained. During staining and finish application, finish fumes require ventilation, so we recommend limiting time in treated rooms for 24–48 hours. We'll tell you exactly what to expect before we start.

Solid hardwood (3/4" thick) can typically be refinished 7–10 times over its life. Engineered hardwood depends on the wear layer — usually 2–4 times. We measure your existing floor wear layer at estimate so you know where you stand before committing.

Spokane's dry winters drop indoor humidity significantly — wood shrinks and boards pull apart slightly, causing gaps and squeaks. This is normal seasonal movement, not a defect. We can address squeaks structurally, and installation jobs we do include proper gapping allowances to accommodate Spokane's climate swing.

Oil-based polyurethane adds a warm amber tone and is very durable — popular for traditional wood looks. Water-based finish dries clear, preserving the natural wood color, and dries faster (back on floors in 24 hrs vs. 48+). Both are excellent — the right choice depends on your wood species and the look you want. We'll walk you through both at estimate.
