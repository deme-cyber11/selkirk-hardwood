Hardwood Floor Refinishing Cost in Spokane: 2026 Pricing Guide

February 21, 2026 · 5 min read

Planning to refinish your hardwood floors in Spokane? Understanding the costs involved helps you budget accurately and avoid surprises. In this comprehensive guide, we'll break down exactly what hardwood floor refinishing costs in the Spokane area, what factors influence pricing, and how to get the best value for your investment.

Average Refinishing Costs in Spokane

In the Spokane metro area, hardwood floor refinishing typically costs between $3 and $8 per square foot, depending on the scope of work. For a typical 1,200-square-foot home with hardwood throughout the main living areas (roughly 800 sq ft of hardwood), you can expect to pay between $2,400 and $6,400.

Here's a general breakdown of what you might pay:

  • Basic sand & refinish (3 coats): $3–$5/sq ft
  • Sand, stain & refinish: $4–$6/sq ft
  • Dustless sanding with premium finish: $5–$8/sq ft
  • Spot repair + refinish: $6–$10/sq ft (smaller areas)

These prices include labor, materials, and finish. Spokane pricing tends to be 15–25% lower than Seattle or Portland due to lower overhead costs, making it an excellent time to invest in your floors.

Factors That Affect Your Refinishing Cost

Every project is different. Here are the main factors that influence what you'll pay:

1. Square Footage

Larger projects typically cost less per square foot because setup and mobilization costs are spread across more area. A 300 sq ft room might run $5–$7/sq ft, while a 1,500 sq ft whole-house project could come in at $3.50–$5/sq ft.

2. Floor Condition

Floors with deep scratches, pet damage, water stains, or worn-through finish require more sanding passes and prep work. If boards need to be replaced before refinishing, that adds $8–$15 per board.

3. Stain vs. Natural Finish

Adding a custom stain color adds $1–$2/sq ft to the total cost. Natural (clear coat) finishes are the most affordable option, while custom color matching or trending dark stains require additional coats and drying time. Check out our guide to the best hardwood species for the Pacific Northwest to understand how wood type affects stain options.

4. Finish Type

Water-based polyurethane (like Bona Traffic HD) costs more than oil-based alternatives but dries faster, has lower VOC emissions, and won't yellow over time. At Selkirk Hardwood, we primarily use water-based finishes for their superior durability and faster return-to-use time.

5. Dustless vs. Traditional Sanding

Dustless sanding systems capture 99%+ of sanding dust, keeping your home cleaner and reducing post-project cleanup. This premium service typically adds $1–$2/sq ft but is well worth it — especially if you're living in the home during the project.

6. Furniture Moving & Prep

Most refinishing companies charge extra for furniture moving, or include it in a slightly higher per-foot rate. At Selkirk Hardwood, basic furniture moving is included in our estimates.

How Spokane Compares to Other PNW Cities

Spokane homeowners benefit from lower labor and operating costs compared to western Washington:

  • Spokane: $3–$8/sq ft
  • Seattle: $5–$12/sq ft
  • Portland: $4–$10/sq ft
  • Boise: $3–$7/sq ft

Money-Saving Tips

  • Refinish the whole house at once — per-square-foot costs drop significantly for larger projects
  • Book in winter or early spring — contractors are less busy and may offer better rates
  • Choose a natural finish — skipping stain saves $1–$2/sq ft
  • Handle furniture moving yourself — save $200–$500
  • Get multiple quotes — but compare apples to apples (same finish, same number of coats)

When to Refinish vs. Replace

If your floors still have enough wood thickness above the tongue (typically 3/4" solid hardwood can be refinished 4–7 times), refinishing is almost always the better value. Replacement runs $8–$15/sq ft installed — two to three times the cost of refinishing. Read our detailed refinish vs. replace guide for help deciding.
