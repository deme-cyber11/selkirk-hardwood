Water Damage Is the #1 Hardwood Repair Issue in Spokane

The Pacific Northwest's moisture-heavy environment means water damage to hardwood floors is far more common in Spokane than most other regions. Spring snowmelt saturates the ground and can enter basements and lower levels through foundation cracks. Frozen pipes during Spokane's subzero winter stretches burst and flood living spaces. Roof ice dams create slow leaks that damage upper-floor hardwood. And the region's frequent rain events can overwhelm window seals and exterior drainage.

The good news: most water-damaged hardwood can be repaired rather than replaced. The key is acting quickly â€” wood that sits wet for more than 48 hours is far more likely to require full board replacement than wood that's dried professionally within hours. If you have active water on your hardwood floors, call us immediately.

Hardwood Repair Services We Offer

Water Damage Restoration

When caught early, cupped and buckled boards can be restored through professional drying, sanding, and refinishing. We use industrial dehumidifiers and moisture meters to dry the subfloor and flooring completely before any repair work begins. Boards with permanent structural damage â€” severe warping, delamination, or mold â€” are replaced with matching material.

Board Replacement

Damaged boards from water, impact, pet urine, or structural failure are carefully removed and replaced with matching species, profile, and width. We source matching material, cut and fit replacements, then sand and finish the repair area to blend with the surrounding floor. Our craftsmen make repairs that are nearly invisible from normal viewing distance.

Gap Filling & Seasonal Gaps

Spokane's dry winters cause hardwood to contract, opening gaps between boards that close again in summer. For truly seasonal gaps, we recommend maintaining 35â€“45% indoor humidity with a humidifier rather than filling gaps that will close on their own. For permanent year-round gaps, we use flexible, stainable wood filler that moves with the wood. Severely gapped floors may require re-laying or weave-in repairs.

Squeaky Floor Repair

Squeaks in Spokane homes are typically caused by seasonal wood movement â€” boards rub against each other or the subfloor as they expand and contract. We diagnose the exact cause and apply the right fix: face-nailing, subfloor fastening, glue injection, or bridging from below.

Pet Damage Repair

Surface scratches from pet nails are removed during standard refinishing. Urine that has penetrated into raw wood creates permanent stains that require board replacement. We'll assess your damage and tell you exactly what's possible.
