Hardwood Floor Refinishing in Spokane, WA — Done Right, Guaranteed

Hardwood floor refinishing restores scratched, worn, and dull floors to like-new condition — sanding down to fresh wood and building a new finish from scratch. Most Spokane homeowners need it when the finish has clouded, deep scratches have broken through the surface, or floors look flat and lifeless despite regular cleaning. Done correctly, refinishing is indistinguishable from new installation. Done wrong — wrong grit sequence, wrong finish for the Inland Northwest climate, no dust control — you get an uneven surface that fails early and looks worse than before.

When You Need Refinishing

Hardwood refinishing becomes necessary when the protective finish layer is compromised — and in Spokane's climate, that happens faster than most homeowners expect. Signs your floors need refinishing:

  • Gray or white scratches that cut through the finish into bare wood — once wood is exposed, every footstep grinds in more grit
  • Dull, flat appearance even after cleaning — the finish has oxidized and no longer reflects light
  • Water rings or dark staining from moisture that penetrated the finish layer
  • Finish peeling or flaking — common on floors that were coated over without proper surface prep
  • Boards that look gray along edges or in traffic lanes — oxidized wood that's been exposed to air

What happens if you wait: scratches go from finish-deep to wood-deep. Wood-deep damage absorbs oils, moisture, and grit from every footstep. Spokane's low-humidity winters pull moisture out of damaged boards faster than intact ones, accelerating checking and cracking. A floor that refinishes cleanly today requires significantly more sanding — and costs more — a year from now.

How Refinishing Works

Step 1 — Free In-Home Assessment: We measure wear layer thickness, check species and condition, and give you a written quote. We tell you upfront whether full refinishing or screen-and-recoat is the right call for your specific floor.

Step 2 — Dustless Sanding: Our HEPA-filtered system captures sanding dust at the source. Three to four progressive grits produce a flat, scratch-free surface. Edge and corner work is done by hand — no machine rounding at walls.

Step 3 — Stain Selection: We apply test patches on your actual floor in your actual lighting. You approve the color before we proceed. Over 40 options — from natural clear to deep walnut. Spokane homeowners favor warm tones: honey oak, warm gray, and weathered driftwood work well with the region's Craftsman and ranch aesthetics.

Step 4 — Finish Coats: Two to three coats of commercial-grade polyurethane, buffed lightly between each. Water-based options cure faster and don't amber over time. Oil-based adds warmth and depth. Spokane's dry air speeds up waterborne cure — light foot traffic at 24 hours, furniture back at 72.

What Does Refinishing Cost in Spokane?

Hardwood floor refinishing in Spokane runs $3.50–$7 per square foot for a full refinish (sand + stain + 3 coats). A 300 sq ft living room runs $1,050–$2,100. A full home at 1,200 sq ft typically runs $4,200–$8,400. Screen-and-recoat (no full sanding — for floors in solid structural condition) costs $1.50–$2.50 per square foot. What drives cost higher: stain color change, stairs and tight spaces, heavily worn floors that need extra sanding passes, or premium finish systems. We tell you which applies before any work starts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I need full refinishing or just a screen-and-recoat?

Screen-and-recoat works when the existing finish is intact — no peeling, no scratches through to bare wood, no stain discoloration you want to change. It scuffs up the existing finish and adds new coats on top. Full refinishing sands to bare wood — the only option when the finish is compromised or you want a different stain color. We assess this during the free estimate so you don't pay for more than you need.

How long does refinishing take?

Most projects take 3–5 days: one day sanding, one for stain (if applicable), then 2–3 days for finish coats with drying between each. Spokane's dry air speeds up waterborne cure — light foot traffic at 24 hours, furniture back at 72 hours, area rugs after 7 days, felt pads on furniture legs after 30 days.

Will there be a lot of dust in my home?

Not with our dustless system. Our HEPA-filtered equipment captures the vast majority of sanding dust at the source. Most Spokane families stay home during the project. With traditional refinishing, fine wood particles get pulled into forced-air ductwork and circulate through your home for months — our system prevents that entirely.

Do I need to be home?

No, but many homeowners prefer to be. You can't use the rooms being worked on, but the rest of the house is fully livable during the project. We recommend being home for the stain selection step so you can approve colors in person before we commit.
